Adaptive surfaces

One coaching note, not four fragments.

The adaptation moment replaces scattered labels like Why this changed, reduced-load notes, drift downgrade summaries, and accepted-change pills with one reusable component. It leads with a calm sentence, opens inline, and keeps Today, Program, Log, and Coach on the same story.

Mode cards follow here

Preview cards can still explain proposed moves, but the adaptation moment becomes the single current-plan explanation once a change is live.

Component rule

One visible sentence, then earned detail.

Collapsed

Icon, source label, and one sentence only.

Expanded

Three short lines: what changed, why now, and what stays protected or what earns next.

Count

One adaptation moment per surface context. Summarize to the strongest coaching reason.

Kinds

Copy changes by kind, not by screen.

  • Reduced load: say what stayed and what came down.
  • Protect: frame protection as intentional coaching, not alarm.
  • Drift downgrade: simplify calmly and explain what earns the tighter fit back.
  • Coach accepted: acknowledge the chosen change without audit language.
  • Carry forward: explain what moved and what did not get doubled up.
Placement

Put it where the user is already orienting.

  • Today: under the hero, above the session breakdown.
  • Program: in the hero, above trajectory.
  • Log: under the planned-session summary, before actuals.
  • Coach: in the top quiet panel, before mode cards.
