# FORMA Launch Positioning Options

## Scope

This memo compares three launch-positioning directions for FORMA against a current running-only market comparator, Running Genie.

The goal is not to copy competitor language.

The goal is to identify the sharpest wedge FORMA can credibly own today based on current repo reality:

- hybrid planning depth
- cross-surface consistency
- structured logging
- adaptation after logs
- honest support boundaries

## Comparator Read: Running Genie

Running Genie is currently positioned as a running-only AI coach centered on:

- race-specific running plans
- Strava-powered personalization
- adaptive running adjustments
- training-load and pace analytics
- running challenges and leaderboards

Official sources used:

- Running Genie website: https://therunninggenie.com/
- Running Genie App Store listing: https://apps.apple.com/in/app/running-genie-ai-coach/id6742008915

The important strategic takeaway is not "beat them at running."

The takeaway is:

- they already have a clean running-only story
- their promise is narrow and easy to understand
- FORMA should not launch by sounding like a broader, fuzzier version of a running app

## Repo Reality That Should Shape Positioning

What FORMA can say more credibly than a running-only app:

- it can plan across run, strength, nutrition, and recovery as one system
- it already has explicit hybrid planning architecture in the repo
- it already has workout and nutrition adaptation proof in local and fresh-device flows
- it already has cross-surface consistency work across Today, Program, Log, Nutrition, and Coach

What FORMA should not overclaim:

- fully proven cross-device restore in every signed-in state
- first-class visible-abs or physique verification
- live reminders
- universal Apple Health or Garmin readiness
- maximal progress in endurance, strength, and fat loss all at once

## Recommendation

Best wedge today: `Option 1 - pure hybrid run+strength coach`

Why:

- It is the clearest differentiation from Running Genie.
- It matches the strongest current repo truth.
- It stays closer to what FORMA already does deterministically: one plan across run, lift, nutrition, recovery, and adaptation.
- It does not force the product to overclaim around broad lifestyle operating-system ambition or physique certainty.

Second-best option: `Option 3`

Weakest option for launch: `Option 2`

Option 2 is intellectually attractive, and the repo language already leans there internally, but it is too broad for launch. It sounds bigger than the current mobile trust boundary.

## Option 1 - Pure Hybrid Run+Strength Coach

## Fit assessment

Best current fit.

This is the sharpest wedge because it attacks the exact gap left by running-only apps: serious people who want to run well without letting strength disappear.

It also gives FORMA a clean answer to "why not just use a running app?" without sounding defensive.

## Homepage hero

**Train for your run without training like running is your only goal.**

FORMA builds one adaptive plan for running, lifting, fueling, and recovery so you can get faster without letting strength slide.

## App Store subtitle

Adaptive run plus strength coach

## Onboarding promise

Tell FORMA what race, running target, or strength standard matters most, and it will build a week that keeps both lanes real instead of pretending you can max everything at once.

## Why choose FORMA over a running-only app

Choose FORMA if you do not want a great run plan that quietly turns strength into random leftovers. FORMA is built for people who want the run to improve while lifts, fueling, and recovery still stay visible in the same system.

## Strengths

- cleanest wedge versus Running Genie
- maps directly to current hybrid planning architecture
- naturally supports run-first and strength-first hybrid stories
- gives Nutrition a real supporting role instead of making it feel bolted on

## Risks

- narrower than FORMA's eventual ambition
- can undersell pure strength users if used too rigidly

## Option 2 - Goal-Based Fitness Operating System For Serious Everyday Athletes

## Fit assessment

Strong internal framing, weaker launch framing.

This is close to how the repo already thinks about itself, but it is too expansive for a first public wedge. It risks sounding abstract, enterprise-like, or overbuilt before the market understands the core behavior.

## Homepage hero

**One system for what to do today - and what changes next.**

FORMA turns goals, training, nutrition, recovery, and real logs into one adaptive plan for serious everyday athletes.

## App Store subtitle

Adaptive fitness planning system

## Onboarding promise

Give FORMA your goals, baselines, and constraints, and it will turn them into a structured weekly plan that updates when your real training does.

## Why choose FORMA over a running-only app

Choose FORMA if your training life does not fit in one lane. Running apps can coach the run. FORMA is for people juggling performance, strength, body-composition pressure, and day-to-day training reality in one place.

## Strengths

- aligns with internal product architecture
- leaves room for future breadth
- makes Today, Program, Log, Nutrition, and Coach feel like one product

## Risks

- too broad for launch
- "operating system" can sound like product theory, not a user benefit
- invites scrutiny on every not-yet-real surface
- weaker immediate contrast with Running Genie

## Option 3 - Adaptive Coach For People Training Toward A Visible Performance Target

## Fit assessment

Good, but needs careful wording.

This option is sharper than Option 2 because it centers on measurable outcomes. It also captures why FORMA feels more serious than generic fitness apps.

But it is still weaker than Option 1 because "visible performance target" can drift toward physique language, and the repo is not honest enough yet to market visible-abs outcomes as first-class operational support.

## Homepage hero

**Train toward a target you can name.**

FORMA helps you build toward a real performance marker like a race time, lift number, or bodyweight milestone, then adjusts the plan when real life changes the week.

## App Store subtitle

Adaptive coach for real training targets

## Onboarding promise

Pick a target that matters, set the baselines you actually know, and FORMA will build a plan that shows what is prioritized, what is held back, and why.

## Why choose FORMA over a running-only app

Choose FORMA if your training target is bigger than just your next run. FORMA connects the run, the lift, the recovery, and the daily execution needed to move a real marker, not just stack miles.

## Strengths

- serious and outcome-oriented
- fits FORMA's honesty work around explicit goals and pace judgment
- less broad than "fitness operating system"

## Risks

- still somewhat abstract
- easy to drift into unsupported physique promises
- weaker immediate category signal than hybrid run-plus-strength

## Winner And Suggested Direction

## Recommended launch position

`FORMA is the adaptive run-plus-strength coach for people who care about both.`

That should be the core wedge.

## Why this wins

- It is easy to understand in one sentence.
- It is meaningfully different from Running Genie.
- It matches the strongest repo-backed behavior.
- It gives FORMA permission to talk about nutrition and recovery as support systems, not separate categories.
- It avoids pretending FORMA is already a universal training operating system for every athlete type.

## Suggested final copy set

## Homepage hero

**Train for your run without training like running is your only goal.**

One adaptive plan for running, lifting, fueling, and recovery - built for people who want to get faster and stay strong.

## App Store subtitle

Adaptive run plus strength coach

## Onboarding promise

Set your running target, strength baseline, and real-world constraints. FORMA builds a week that makes the priority visible, keeps the support work alive, and adjusts when your training changes.

## Why choose FORMA over a running-only app

Running apps help you run more. FORMA helps you train like a complete athlete when the run still matters most.

## Copy Guardrails

Do say:

- run plus strength
- adaptive weekly plan
- one plan across training, fueling, and recovery
- serious everyday athletes who care about both
- clear tradeoffs instead of fake max-everything promises

Do not say:

- all-in-one operating system on the public homepage as the lead claim
- visible abs as a lead promise
- fully connected device ecosystem
- reminders keep you on track
- train smarter with your data
- AI running coach

Those last two drift too close to the current running-only category language and make FORMA sound less distinct.

## Bottom Line

Running Genie is narrow, clear, and running-only.

FORMA should answer with a wedge that is just as clear:

not "everything fitness"
not "AI for all athletes"
not "visible abs plus PRs plus perfect recovery"

The strongest launch claim FORMA can credibly own today is:

`the adaptive run-plus-strength coach for serious people who want both`
